It can often be confusing when learning the difference between Design Methodologies and Design Methods. Much of this comes from different points of view between different fields; art, design, engineering, science; as well as even differing opinions between institutions. Not all things are the same to all people.

This guide outlines one designers point of view, with short guides and useful links when considering what kind methods or methodologies to choose when beginning a project.

Methodologies are frameworks which you can follow through one/several steps or the entire design process. They generally have definitive stages which help you to follow a structured, practised procedure.

Methods are the tools which we use within the stages of our chosen methodologies. Each method usually has a specific stage where they are commonly used, however they are also very versatile and can often be used throughout your design process to achieve different goals.
